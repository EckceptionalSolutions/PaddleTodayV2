# Michigan Route Memory

Last summarized: 2026-05-31.

## Current Inventory

- Live routes: 19.
- Top represented rivers: Shiawassee River 9, Clinton River 6, Pine River 3, Huron River 1.
- Ledger candidates: 94 total; 19 added, 32 `threshold_weak`, 20 `no_live_gauge`, 10 `research_later`, 6 `gauge_proxy_weak`, 4 `blocked_until_date`, 3 `rejected`.

## Status

Michigan is not exhausted, but the easy wins are mostly picked over. The current catalog is concentrated in Shiawassee, Clinton, Pine, and one Huron route. Many remaining Michigan candidates are real paddling corridors but lack enough threshold, live-gauge, access, or endpoint confidence to ship.

## Main Blockers

- Weak numeric thresholds on popular flatwater/water-trail corridors.
- No live gauge on many Upper Peninsula and smaller whitewater leads.
- Gauge proxy weakness where a nearby river or downstream dammed gauge does not safely represent the route.
- Temporary closures: Huron River Barton/Argo corridor, Rogue River Grand Rogue launches, lower Boardman downtown work, Grand Rapids whitewater project.
- Some AW whitewater/play features are rejected or deferred because they are not normal PaddleToday route records.

## Current Guidance

- Pause broad cadence.
- Run focused candidate work only when a known blocker is resolved.
- Highest-value future research is finding official or strong local threshold sources for major water trails: Huron, Au Sable, Grand, Kalamazoo, Paw Paw, Black, Red Cedar, Muskegon, Rifle, and Lower Grand families.
- Keep access closures and dam/portage details first-class; do not infer public endpoints from map pins alone.
